About the Project

Mission & Vision

Our virtual museum aims to create an engaging online platform showcasing historical repressions, providing accessible digital exhibitions and educational resources. We strive to preserve historical memory and promote understanding through comprehensive research and interactive presentations.

Project Significance

This digital archive serves as a vital resource for researchers, educators, and the general public interested in understanding the complex history of political and social repressions. Our platform offers unprecedented access to documents, testimonies, and scholarly analysis.

Project Team

Dr. Sarah Chen
Project Director

Education: Ph.D. in Modern History, Harvard University (2008); M.A. in Digital Humanities, Stanford University (2004)

Specialization: 20th-century political repressions, digital archiving, comparative studies of authoritarian regimes

Experience: 15 years of research in historical repressions, leading positions in several international archival projects. Author of three monographs on political repressions in Central Europe.

Publications: Over 40 peer-reviewed articles, editor of special issue of Journal of Contemporary History dedicated to digital methods in historical research.

Contact: s.chen@virtualmuseum.org

Prof. Michael Rodriguez
Lead Historian

Education: Ph.D. in European History, Oxford University (1995); M.A. in Political Science, Columbia University (1991)

Specialization: Totalitarian regimes, oral history, social memory and trauma, historical research methodology

Experience: 25 years of academic practice, professor at University of California Berkeley. Consultant for Truth and Reconciliation Commissions in several countries.

Publications: Author of 6 books including the award-winning monograph "Voices from the Shadows: Oral History and Political Repression" (2018). Over 80 scholarly articles.

Awards: Guggenheim Fellowship (2015), American Historical Association Award for Excellence in Teaching (2020)
